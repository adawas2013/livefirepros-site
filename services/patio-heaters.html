About This Service

Warmth Where You Want It, When You Need It

Southern California has the best weather in the country — but even here, winter evenings drop into the 40s and 50s, and coastal breezes can cut through a patio gathering fast. A well-placed patio heater turns a three-season outdoor space into a four-season one, keeping your family comfortable at dinner and your guests lingering long after dark.

At LiveFirePros, we install patio heating systems for both residential and commercial clients across North County San Diego, greater San Diego, Orange County, and Temecula Valley. Whether you need a single heater for a small balcony or a full multi-zone heating system for a restaurant patio, we'll design the right solution and handle the complete installation — fuel supply, electrical, mounting, and controls.

Gas patio heaters are the most popular option for larger residential patios and commercial spaces. Freestanding mushroom-style heaters run on propane tanks and deliver wide, powerful heat to a 15–20 foot radius. For a cleaner look, we install permanent gas heaters connected to your home's natural gas supply — no tanks to refill, no clutter, just reliable heat on demand.

Electric infrared heaters are the go-to choice for covered patios, pergolas, and enclosed outdoor rooms. Wall-mounted and ceiling-mounted electric units produce instant, directional warmth using infrared technology — they heat people and objects directly rather than heating the air, which makes them highly efficient even on windy evenings. They're silent, produce no emissions, and can be controlled by remote, timer, or smart-home integration.

Built-in heating systems take the concept further with recessed ceiling heaters, in-ground radiant panels, or custom heating elements integrated into pergola beams and patio structures. These systems are invisible when off and powerfully effective when on — the kind of premium installation that separates an average patio from a resort-quality outdoor room.

For our commercial clients — restaurants, hotels, event venues, and bars — we design and install heater layouts that maximize coverage while meeting fire safety codes and ADA requirements. We understand the specific challenges of heating open-air dining spaces: wind exposure, ceiling heights, power availability, and the need for reliable, low-maintenance equipment that runs every night of the season.

It depends on your space. Electric infrared heaters are best for covered patios, pergolas, and enclosed areas — they're silent, produce no emissions, and can be mounted to walls or ceilings for a clean look. Gas heaters (propane or natural gas) deliver more raw heat and work better in open, uncovered spaces where wind is a factor. If you have a covered patio and value convenience, electric is usually our recommendation. For large open areas and commercial settings, gas mushroom heaters or built-in natural gas units are typically more effective.

A general rule of thumb is about 10 BTUs per square foot for covered patios and 20+ BTUs per square foot for open, wind-exposed areas. A typical mushroom gas heater produces 40,000–48,000 BTUs and effectively heats a 15–20 foot diameter area. Wall-mounted electric heaters range from 1,500 to 6,000 watts (approximately 5,000–20,000 BTUs). During our consultation, we measure your space and account for ceiling height, wind, and seating layout to calculate exactly what you need.

Electric infrared heaters are perfectly suited for covered patios and pergolas — they produce no combustion gases and can be safely mounted to ceilings and walls. Gas mushroom heaters require adequate ventilation and minimum ceiling clearance (typically 24+ inches above the reflector). We never install gas mushroom heaters in fully enclosed spaces. For covered patios with low ceilings, we always recommend electric infrared as the safest, most practical option.
