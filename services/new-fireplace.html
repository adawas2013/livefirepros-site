About This Service

A Fireplace Changes Everything

There's a reason a fireplace is consistently ranked as one of the most desired home features by buyers and homeowners alike. It's not just about heat — it's about creating a focal point that anchors an entire room, a place where families gather on cool evenings and guests naturally drift toward during a party. A fireplace adds architectural character, emotional warmth, and real resale value to your home.

At LiveFirePros, we specialize in installing new fireplaces in homes that have never had one — whether you're building from the ground up, converting an unused room, or adding a statement piece to a living room, bedroom, or covered patio. Our team handles the full scope of work: structural assessment, framing, venting, gas line installation, finish carpentry, and final styling. You get a single team managing everything from the first sketch to the final flame.

We work with all major fuel types. Gas fireplaces are our most popular choice for Southern California homeowners — they deliver instant ambiance with a flip of a switch, no ash cleanup, and precise temperature control. Direct-vent gas units pull combustion air from outside and exhaust through a sealed system, making them safe and efficient for bedrooms, bathrooms, and tight spaces.

Electric fireplaces are the simplest to install and work beautifully in condos, apartments, or rooms where venting isn't practical. Modern linear electric units produce surprisingly realistic flame effects and can be recessed into a wall for a sleek, contemporary look — no gas line or chimney required.

For homeowners who want the authentic crackle and aroma of a real wood fire, we build wood-burning fireplaces with proper masonry, firebrick lining, and code-compliant chimney systems. These are more involved installations, but the result is a timeless centerpiece that connects you to something primal and irreplaceable.

Every installation starts with a thorough home assessment. We evaluate your wall structure, check for existing gas lines, review local building codes, and discuss your design vision. Then we present a clear plan with material options, a realistic timeline, and transparent pricing — no surprises halfway through the project.

Most gas fireplaces we install are direct-vent, which means they use a sealed, dual-pipe system — one pipe draws fresh air in from outside, and the other exhausts combustion gases out. This can vent horizontally through a wall or vertically through the roof. Vent-free (ventless) units are also available and don't require any venting at all, though they have room-size limitations under California regulations. We'll recommend the best option for your specific situation.
