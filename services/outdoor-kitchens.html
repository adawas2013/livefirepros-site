About This Service

Your Kitchen, Under the Open Sky

In Southern California, the backyard isn't just extra space — it's where life happens. An outdoor kitchen takes that reality and elevates it, turning your patio into a place where you can grill a steak, mix a cocktail, plate a salad, and never leave the party to go inside. It's the single biggest upgrade you can make to your outdoor living experience.

At LiveFirePros, we design and build outdoor kitchens that work as hard as your indoor kitchen — but look even better. Every build starts with your lifestyle. Do you host large groups and need a spacious island with bar seating? Are you a serious griller who wants a 42-inch built-in with a dedicated smoker and side burner? Or are you looking for a compact, elegant island that fits a smaller patio without overwhelming the space? We've built all of them.

Our outdoor kitchens are constructed with steel-framed structures clad in your choice of stone, stucco, tile, or brick veneer. This gives you the look of solid masonry with a lighter, faster, and more cost-effective build. The frames are rust-resistant and rated for outdoor exposure, and the cladding options are virtually unlimited — natural stack stone for a rustic look, smooth stucco for modern lines, or porcelain tile for a clean, contemporary finish.

We work with all the major appliance brands — Lynx, DCS, Bull, Blaze, Summerset, and more — and can source grills, side burners, pizza ovens, warming drawers, refrigerators, ice makers, and sinks that fit your budget and cooking style. Want a built-in kegerator? A charcoal kamado alongside your gas grill? A wood-fired pizza oven? We've done it all.

Countertops are where form meets function. We install granite, quartz, concrete, and porcelain slab countertops that stand up to heat, rain, and UV without staining, cracking, or fading. Every countertop is cut and finished on-site for a seamless fit around your appliances and sink.

Utility work is included in every project. We handle gas line routing from your meter to the island, electrical for lighting and outlets, and plumbing for sinks and drainage. If you want lighting integrated into the island — LED strips under the counter, pendant lights over the bar — we coordinate that too. One team, one project, no juggling subcontractors.

How It Works

Our Process

01

Consultation

We tour your outdoor space, discuss how you cook and entertain, review utility access points, and take measurements. You'll leave the meeting with a clear idea of what's possible within your budget.

02

Design

We create a layout with appliance selections, material samples, and a comprehensive proposal. You'll see the island configuration, countertop options, and a detailed cost breakdown before we break ground.

03

Build

Our crew builds the steel frame, runs gas, plumbing, and electrical, applies the cladding and countertops, and installs all appliances. Most outdoor kitchens are completed in 2–4 weeks.

04

Walkthrough

We fire up every appliance, test every connection, walk you through operation and care, and make sure you're thrilled with every detail. Then it's time for your first cook.

Granite and porcelain slabs are our top recommendations for Southern California outdoor kitchens. Granite is naturally heat-resistant, extremely durable, and comes in a wide range of colors and patterns. Porcelain slabs are virtually maintenance-free — they don't stain, don't fade in UV, and resist heat from hot pans. Concrete is another popular choice for homeowners who want a more industrial or modern look. We avoid materials like marble and some quartz brands that can discolor or crack with sustained outdoor exposure.

In most jurisdictions across San Diego and Orange County, yes — outdoor kitchens that involve gas lines, plumbing, and electrical work require permits. If you're adding a pergola or shade structure, that may require a separate structural permit. We handle the entire permit process for you, including submitting plans, scheduling inspections, and making sure everything passes. You won't need to visit the permit office or coordinate with inspectors yourself.

We work with all the major outdoor appliance manufacturers including Lynx, DCS, Bull, Blaze, Summerset, Lion, Delta Heat, and Alfresco.
