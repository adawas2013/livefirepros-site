About This Service

The Grandest Fire Feature You Can Build

An outdoor fireplace isn't just a fire feature — it's architecture. It's the kind of element that defines an entire outdoor space, creating a vertical focal point that draws the eye, anchors the seating layout, and makes your patio feel like an extension of your home rather than an afterthought. Where fire pits invite you to look down and gather around, an outdoor fireplace invites you to look up and settle in.

At LiveFirePros, we've been designing and building outdoor fireplaces across Southern California for years. We understand how to scale these structures to your patio — a fireplace that's too small looks insignificant, while one that's too large overwhelms the space. Getting the proportions right is everything, and it's where our experience shows.

We build two main categories of outdoor fireplaces. Custom masonry fireplaces are built from scratch using real brick, natural stone, or manufactured stone veneer over a concrete block core. These are substantial, permanent structures with full chimneys — the kind that become the defining feature of a property. They can be finished in stacked ledgestone for a rustic mountain feel, smooth plaster for a Mediterranean look, or natural flagstone for something uniquely organic.

Prefabricated outdoor fireplaces use a factory-built firebox and chimney system that we enclose in a custom-built surround. This approach costs less than full masonry and installs faster, but the finished result looks just as impressive. The surround can be faced in the same natural stone, brick, or stucco as a masonry build — most guests won't know the difference.

Fuel choice matters outdoors. Gas outdoor fireplaces offer instant ignition, no smoke to annoy neighbors, and a clean flame that's consistent in any wind. They're ideal for covered patios where smoke would be a problem and for homeowners who want the ambiance without the tending. Wood-burning outdoor fireplaces deliver the full sensory experience — crackling logs, shifting embers, and the smoky aroma that makes outdoor evenings feel like a mountain retreat.

Every outdoor fireplace we build includes proper foundation work, chimney construction or installation, spark arrestors, and compliance with local setback and clearance codes. We coordinate with your HOA if required, pull all necessary permits, and manage inspections from start to sign-off.

What We Offer

Outdoor Fireplace Options

🧱 Custom Masonry

Full brick-and-mortar or stone construction with a concrete block core and real chimney. The most impressive and permanent outdoor fireplace you can build — a true architectural element.

🏗️ Prefab with Custom Finish

A factory-built firebox and flue system wrapped in a custom stone, stucco, or brick surround. Faster to install, lower cost, and visually indistinguishable from full masonry when finished.

🔥 Gas Outdoor Fireplaces

Smokeless, instant-on convenience with realistic log sets and adjustable flame. Perfect for covered patios, close-neighbor situations, and homeowners who want zero-maintenance fire.

🪵 Wood-Burning Outdoor

The real thing — a full wood-burning fireplace with proper chimney, spark arrestor, and ash management. For those who believe there's no substitute for the crackle and glow of real wood.

📐 See-Through & Two-Sided

Double-sided outdoor fireplaces that serve two seating areas — one facing each direction. An elegant way to divide and define separate zones on a large patio.

🛋️ Integrated Seating Walls

Matching stone or stucco seating walls that wrap around the fireplace, creating a built-in lounge zone. Add cushions and throws and you've got the coziest spot in the house — outside.

How It Works

Our Process

01

Consultation

We visit your patio, evaluate the space, discuss your vision, and assess structural and code requirements. We'll recommend the right size, style, and fuel type based on your specific situation.

Gas is the better choice if your outdoor fireplace is under a covered patio, close to neighbors, or if you want instant fire without prep or cleanup. Wood-burning is the way to go if you love the full campfire experience and your fireplace is in an open area with good clearance. Many homeowners in San Diego choose gas for convenience and to avoid smoke complaints. We'll help you weigh the pros and cons based on your specific patio setup and local regulations.

Outdoor fireplace chimneys must typically extend a minimum of 2 feet above any structure within 10 feet, and the overall chimney height must be sufficient for proper draft. Wood-burning units require a spark arrestor at the top. Clearance from combustible materials — overhanging roofs, pergola beams, fences — varies by jurisdiction but generally requires a minimum of 3–5 feet. We design every outdoor fireplace to meet or exceed these requirements and handle all inspections.
